Welcome

  • Thank you for participating in this experiment!
  • This experiment shouldn't take more than 15 minutes and you could earn substantial money.
  • Clear off your desk completely - only a pen (not a pencil) may remain.
  • Switch off your cell phones and put them in your bag.

No Talking No Communicating:

  • • Do NOT COMMUNICATE with the other participants at any point.
  • • Keep your handouts and answers concealed from your fellow subjects.
If you have Questions at any point:
  • At any time, you can raise your hand.
  • An experimenter will come to you.
  • Ask your question quietly and we will answer your question privately.
  • We will announce any necessary clarifications publicly.

Earning Money

  • You will have many opportunities to earn money in this experiment.
  • Your earnings will always be reported in tokens. In cash, each token is worth 25 cents.
  • Your cash earnings will be handed to you by your TA in a private envelope in your next discussion section.

Anonymity

  • This experiment is anonymous
  • We will not collect or record any names or perms.
  • None of your responses or actions will ever be shared during the experiment.
  • We'll give a random ID number to each of you.
  • This ID number is on the top corner of your Instruction Sheet.
  • These numbers are never connected to your name.
  • Even the experimenter doesn't know which ID number belongs to which person!
  • Write your ID on everything you turn in - this ID# is our only way to pay you!

No Deception

  • Economics experiments have a strict policy against deception.
  • If we do anything deceptive or don't pay you as described, then you can complain to the campus Human Subjects Committee.

The Color Task

  • You'll get exactly 10 seconds to look at a grid of 400 colored dots.
  • The dots will be one of two different colors.
  • There will be 10 more dots of one color than of the other.
  • Your task will be to determine which color dot there is more of.

Example: In the grid below, which color dot is there more of?

  • If you thought there were more Purple dots (205 Purple dots vs 195 Orange dots) you'd write "Purple" on your handout
  • If you thought there were more Orange dots(205 Orange dots vs 195 Purple dots) you'd write "Orange" on your handout

  • Then you have 5 seconds to write down your color guess.
  • For each practice grid, you earn 1 token for guessing correctly.
  • Be sure to conceal your guess from your neighbors!

The Likelihood Task Now that you have made your guess about the dot grid, you will be asked to write down a number between 0 and 100% the reflects how likely you think it is that you guessed the previous grid correctly.

Payment Details There are two lotteries on which you can base your earnings: The "Correct-Guess Lottery" or the "Gumball Lottery"

The "Correct-Guess" Lottery: • You win 6 tokens if your guess about the most recent dot grid was correct, but you win 0 tokens if your guess was not correct.

The "Gumball" Lottery:

  • Imagine a gumball machine that has 100 gumballs in it.
  • Each gumball is either black or green.
  • After the experiment, one gumball will be drawn from this machine at random.
  • If that gumball turns out to be green, then you will win a prize of 6 tokens.
  • If the gumball is black, you win zero tokens.

In the next task, you'll write on your handout exactly how many green gumballs would need to be in the machine in order for you to be willing to base your potential earnings on the ''Gumball Lottery'' rather than on the ''Correct-Guess Lottery''. If this seems complicated, remember that this whole payment method is simply a way to ensure that, on average, expressing your true beliefs about the likelihood of a correct guess will earn you the most tokens.

Check-Up Questions Q1) If you believe there's a 30% chance that you guessed the previous grid correctly, how many green gumballs would need to be in the machine in order for you to be willing to play the Gumball Lottery rather than the Correct-Guess Lottery? Q2) Susan thinks about her previous guess and decides that she'd prefer the Gumball Lottery as long as there are at least 64 green gumballs in it.
